Foam vs Memory Foam vs Orthopedic vs Pillow Top: Which Mattress Is Right for You?

By Mattbed Canada  ·  June 2026  ·  Mattress Types Guide

Foam, memory foam, orthopedic, hybrid, pillow top — what does it all mean? This guide breaks it down simply.

1. Foam Mattress (Pure Foam)

The most affordable and straightforward type. Made entirely from high-density polyurethane foam. Gives a firm, flat surface similar to the firm “gadda” or mattress common in South Asia. Available in 5", 7", 9", 10" and 12" thickness. Best for: Budget shoppers, people who prefer a firm feel, children’s rooms.

2. Memory Foam Mattress

Contours to the shape of your body using body heat. Excellent for people with joint pain or back pain as it removes pressure points. Low motion transfer means you won’t feel your partner move. Best for: People with back pain, side sleepers, couples.

3. Orthopedic Mattress

Designed specifically for back and spine support. Typically firmer than standard foam and engineered to keep your spine in neutral alignment. Many South Asian customers prefer orthopedic mattresses as they’re used to firmer sleeping surfaces. Best for: Chronic back pain, firm support preference, older adults.

4. Hybrid Mattress

Combines foam on top with a spring or coil system beneath. You get pressure-relieving comfort of foam plus the bounce and support of springs. Great for hot sleepers as springs allow more airflow. Best for: People who want both comfort and support.

5. Pillow Top Mattress

Has an extra layer of soft padding sewn onto the top — feels like sleeping on a cloud. The most plush and luxurious feel. Significantly softer than orthopedic or foam. Best for: People who prefer a soft, luxurious sleep surface.

Which Mattress Is Best for South Asian Families?

If you grew up sleeping on a firm charpoy or gadda, many Canadian mattresses may feel too soft. We recommend starting with an orthopedic foam mattress or a high-density foam mattress — firm support your body is used to, without the sinking feeling of plush mattresses.

Mattress Thickness Guide: 5", 7", 9", 10" & 12" — Which One Should You Choose?

By Mattbed Canada  ·  June 2026  ·  Mattress Thickness Guide

Thickness affects comfort, support, longevity, and how well your mattress works with your bedframe. Here is a simple guide to every thickness we carry.

5-Inch Mattress

Lightweight, easy to move, very affordable. Works well on platform bedframes. Good for children or spare rooms. Not ideal for heavier adults or those with back pain. Best for: Children’s beds, bunk beds, spare rooms, low-budget setups.

7-Inch Mattress

A step up from the 5-inch. Good budget option for a single adult or student. Works with most bedframes and is still easy to transport. Best for: Single adults, students, guest beds.

9-Inch Mattress ⭐ Most Popular

Our most popular thickness. Hits the sweet spot between affordability, support and comfort. Works with all bedframe types. Best for: Most adults, couples, everyday use — our most recommended thickness.

10-Inch Mattress

Excellent lumbar support, more pressure relief for hips and shoulders, lasts longer. Our memory foam and orthopedic mattresses are often 10 inches. Best for: Couples, heavier adults, people with back pain.

12-Inch Mattress

Our thickest option — a luxury sleep experience. Exceptionally comfortable, highly durable, maximum support and pressure relief. Combines multiple foam layers. Best for: Couples wanting premium comfort, master bedrooms, longest-lasting mattress.

Queen Bedframe Size Guide Canada: What Fits, What Doesn’t, and What to Buy First

By Mattbed Canada  ·  June 2026  ·  Bedframe Guide

If you’re buying a queen mattress, you need a queen bedframe. A Canadian queen bedframe is designed to hold a 60×80 inch mattress. Interior dimensions are typically 60.5–61 inches wide × 80.5–81 inches long, giving a small clearance so the mattress doesn’t feel squeezed.

Types of Bedframes We Deliver

Platform bedframes have a solid or slatted base that supports the mattress directly — no box spring needed. Most popular in Canada today, work with foam, memory foam and orthopedic mattresses. Low to the ground, which many South Asian families prefer.

Metal bedframes are the most affordable option. Simple metal rail with centre support legs. Require a box spring or work with thick foam mattress (9" or more). Very easy to assemble and transport.

Do You Need a Bedframe With a Foam Mattress?

Yes — sleeping directly on the floor doesn’t allow airflow beneath the mattress, which leads to moisture buildup and can damage foam over time. A simple platform or metal frame lifts the mattress and keeps it in good condition. We deliver bedframes same-day alongside your mattress.
