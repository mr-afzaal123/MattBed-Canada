Memory Foam Mattress — What it Is and Who Needs It

Memory foam was originally developed by NASA and works by responding to your body heat and weight — it softens where you press and cradles your body shape. This means it relieves pressure on your hips, shoulders and knees — areas that often ache on a hard mattress.

Memory foam is best for:

  • Side sleepers — your hip and shoulder need cushioning when you sleep on your side. Memory foam prevents pressure build-up on these joints
  • Couples — when one partner moves, memory foam absorbs the movement so the other partner doesn't feel it
  • People with joint pain — knees, hips and shoulders get pressure relief on memory foam
  • People moving from a soft bed — memory foam feels most similar to what most Canadians grew up sleeping on

Memory foam is not ideal for:

  • Back sleepers who need firm support under the lumbar spine
  • People who sleep hot — memory foam retains body heat more than firm foam
  • Families who prefer the hard sleeping surface common in South Asia

Which is Better for Back Pain Specifically?

Back pain is the most common reason Canadians research mattresses. The honest answer is that firmness matters more than mattress type. Research published in Sleep Medicine Reviews found that medium-firm mattresses reduced back pain most effectively regardless of whether they were foam or spring.

✅ For lower back pain — choose orthopedic

If your back pain is in the lower back (lumbar region) and you sleep on your back — an orthopedic firm mattress like the DualFirm will prevent your hips from sinking and keep your spine in a neutral position all night. This is the most common recommendation.

✅ For pressure point pain — choose memory foam

If your pain is in your hips, knees or shoulders — and you sleep on your side — memory foam will relieve the pressure points that cause this pain. The contouring cradles these areas instead of pushing back against them.

What About Temperature — Canada's Cold Winters?

This is an important question for Canadian buyers. Memory foam traps body heat — which can feel uncomfortable in summer but actually comfortable in winter. Orthopedic firm foam does not trap heat and tends to sleep cooler year-round.

For Calgary and Edmonton where temperatures drop to -30°C in winter, many families actually prefer memory foam for winter use.
